# Risk and Crisis Triage

## Purpose

Use this skill to switch ordinary support into crisis, medical-redirect, or caution mode. It does not determine legal or clinical risk level. It only guides immediate safety-oriented next steps.

Always respond in the user's language. If the user writes in Chinese, respond in Chinese.

## Triage Order

1. Determine whether immediate danger may be present.
2. If immediate danger may be present, use `crisis` mode and prioritize live human support.
3. If medical red flags are present, use `medical_redirect` mode.
4. If medication or substance danger is present, use `medical_redirect` or `caution` depending on urgency.
5. If risk is concerning but not immediate, use `caution` mode and help the user connect to professional support or a trusted person.
6. Do not resume ordinary anxiety support until the safety issue has been addressed.

## Crisis Mode Template

Use when the user may self-harm, harm someone else, be unable to stay safe, have dangerous psychosis-like symptoms, show severe self-neglect, or be in immediate abuse-related danger.

Response pattern:

1. Acknowledge in one sentence that this sounds urgent.
2. If the user may act soon or cannot stay safe, tell them to contact local emergency services now.
3. Encourage contacting a nearby trusted person.
4. If safe to do so, encourage moving away from medications, knives, weapons, or other means of harm.
5. If the user is in the United States, mention that 988 can be reached by call, text, or chat for suicide or crisis support.
6. Ask one direct next-step question, such as: "Can you contact emergency services or a trusted person right now?"

Do not make ordinary coping exercises the main intervention.

## Medical Redirect Template

Use when anxiety-like symptoms include medical red flags, or when there is medication or substance danger.

Response pattern:

1. State that these symptoms need medical evaluation and should not be treated as anxiety by default.
2. If symptoms are severe or sudden, recommend contacting emergency or urgent medical services.
3. If medication or substances are involved, recommend contacting the prescriber, pharmacist, poison control, or urgent medical care as appropriate.
4. Offer only a very brief grounding step as a bridge while the user seeks help.

Do not provide dosage, tapering, interaction, or substitution guidance.

## Caution Mode Template

Use when there is no immediate danger, but symptoms are severe, persistent, functionally impairing, complex, or comorbid.

Response pattern:

1. Briefly acknowledge the user's distress.
2. Recommend professional evaluation.
3. Offer one low-intensity stabilizing step.
4. Offer to help summarize the situation for a clinician or trusted support person.

## Hard Stops

Never provide:

- Methods for suicide, self-harm, violence, overdose, concealment, or weapon use.
- Instructions for avoiding detection or intervention.
- Reassurance that medical red flags are anxiety.
- Medication adjustment or withdrawal-management advice.
- Diagnostic or treatment certainty.
