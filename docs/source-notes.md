# Source Notes

This project follows an evidence-first clinical workflow model informed by:

- NICE CG113: Generalised anxiety disorder and panic disorder in adults: management.
  - Stepped care: least intrusive effective intervention first.
  - Assessment should consider distress and functional impairment, not only symptom counts.
  - Low-intensity psychological interventions should be based on CBT principles.
- NICE NG225: Self-harm: assessment, management and preventing recurrence.
  - Do not use risk assessment tools or scales alone to predict future suicide or determine treatment/discharge decisions.
  - Focus on individual needs, safety, and psychosocial assessment.
- NHS Talking Therapies service standards:
  - Routine outcome monitoring.
  - PHQ-9 for depression monitoring and GAD-7 as a default anxiety measure, with disorder-specific measures when appropriate.
- WHO psychological self-help interventions:
  - Evidence-based self-help can expand access to support for depression and anxiety.
  - Self-help should remain within structured intervention frameworks.
- SAMHSA SAFE-T and Columbia C-SSRS concepts:
  - Direct inquiry about suicidal thoughts, plans, behavior, intent, and protective factors.

Design implications:

- Use stepped support.
- Do not diagnose.
- Do not expose internal diagnostic hypotheses; formulation is only for safety routing.
- Do not use scale scores alone to determine safety.
- Treat medical red flags as medical until assessed.
- Crisis disclosures require live human support.
- Every new skill needs a documented clinical basis and limitations.
